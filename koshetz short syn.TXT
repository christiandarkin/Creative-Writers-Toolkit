1919. Austria-Hungary has fallen, Russia is drowning in the chaos of the socialist revolution and from within their former imperial territories new countries spring. ALEXANDER KOSHETZ, the virtuosic Ukrainian conductor and composer is sent to Paris with a diplomatic mission - he is to gather a choir and showcase the richness of Ukrainian culture to the European delegates of the Paris Peace Conference, thus earning the official support and protection for the newly found state - Ukraine - against the bolsheviks. 
Having escaped the civil war at home, Koshetz tours Europe with great success, conquering one capital after another with his music. However, despite the triumph, the country he represents ceases to exist, as the bolsheviks are taking over.
